<div class="eyebrow">Question 1 • Discussion</div>

# Is Dharma Only Religion, Or Is It A Way Of Living?

<!-- speaker-notes:
- Main point: Dharma is bigger than a label; it is a way of living rightly.
- Why it matters: Children often hear the word without seeing how practical and active it really is.
- Example angle: Truth, fairness, and restraint are not abstract ideas; they are daily tests.
- Ask: If dharma is only a label, what changes in actual life?
- Intended takeaway: Dharma becomes meaningful when it shapes conduct.
-->

- **Dharma** is not only identity; it is right order in thought and action.
- **Living** matters because values must become visible in conduct.
- **Clarity** asks what is right, not only what feels easy.

# Why Is Dharma Difficult Even When We Know What Is Right?

<!-- speaker-notes:
- Main point: The difficulty of dharma is not lack of information; it is the cost of courage.
- Why it matters: Young people often know what is right but feel weak in the moment of action.
- Example angle: You can know the truth and still hide from it when the social cost feels high.
- Ask: Which is harder for you: knowing the right thing or doing it?
- Intended takeaway: Dharma needs both clarity and backbone.
-->

- **Knowing** is easier than acting when desire or fear enters the scene.
- **Discipline** protects values exactly when pressure rises.
- **Courage** turns moral clarity into real action.
